## One account, every app

Your Eurosky account is more than a login for Bluesky. It's your identity on the Atmosphere - a growing ecosystem of apps and services that all work together, built on the AT Protocol.

With a single Eurosky account, you can post on Bluesky, share photos on Flashes, publish a professional profile on SIFA, write a blog post on Leaflet, collaborate on code with Tangled, and much more. You never need to create a new account or rebuild your social connections, unless you want to separate your online accounts for privacy (you're not limited to a single Eurosky account). Everything is already there.

## How it works

Traditional social media locks your identity, your followers, and your content inside one app. If you want to try a new platform, you start from zero.

The Atmosphere works differently. Your Eurosky account lives on your own Personal Data Server (PDS), a secure home for your identity and data, hosted in Europe. Apps connect to your PDS rather than the other way around. This means your account (@you.eurosky.social) is recognised everywhere in the Atmosphere, your social graph (who you follow and who follows you) can carry across apps, the content you create is stored on your PDS and accessible to any app you choose, and you can switch between apps freely without losing anything.

Think of your PDS as your digital home, and apps as different rooms you can walk into. You're always you, wherever you go.

## Using Atmosphere apps

_Please note that each Atmosphere app has separate terms of service and privacy policies that you need to agree to._

Just visit any Atmosphere app and sign in with your Eurosky account. For most apps, you'll be asked to enter your account in the format user.eurosky.social, and then you'll be taken to eurosky.social to enter your password. Sometimes an Atmosphere account is referred to as AT Protocol/ATProto/Atmosphere/Internet account/handle. These all mean the same thing.

### Using Bluesky

There is one exception to the above, and that’s Bluesky, which requires a few extra steps when signing in:

1. When you go to Bluesky, you will see this. Click sign in. On the next page click your account (it will show as logged out)<br>![The welcome screen for the website bsky.app](/assets/explore/step-1.webp)
2. On the next page, click on the edit button in the ‘Hosting provider’ field.<br>![The sign in screen for bsky.app with a yellow circle around the edit button for the Hosting provider field, which is what you need to click](/assets/explore/step-2.webp)
3. Select ‘Custom’ and enter ‘eurosky.social’ in the Server address field<br>![The dialog that shows after clicking the 'Hosting provider' edit button, where you need to select "Custom" from the tabs and enter "eurosky.social"](/assets/explore/step-3.webp)
4. Enter your username in the format user.eurosky.social (or your account email address) and your password<br>![After clicking "done" in the dialog, you'll see the Hosting provider listed as 'eurosky.social', and then you need to enter your username and password](/assets/explore/step-4.webp)
